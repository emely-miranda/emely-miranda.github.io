Why do K-pop idols debut so young?

By Emely Miranda-Agular

JOUR352

Introduction

When trying to become a singer, people typically post covers online, submit demos to labels or do whatever they can to get discovered. Then bam! After a year or so, they release a single or an EP and claim their success.

Becoming a singer—especially in K-pop—is slightly different in South Korea. Idols must undergo long training periods, honing their skills and preparing for their debut, a journey not for the faint-hearted and not everyone sees through.

Korean pop, or K-pop, emerged in the 1990s and has since transcended borders, becoming a global sensation of immense influence.

K-pop is divided into generations based on its impact. We are currently in the 5th generation.

The success of previous generations has led companies to debut an abundance of new groups. Between January 2023 and April 2024, over 60 K-pop groups have debuted.

Excluding duos and groups not classified as pop, approximately 53 groups debuted during the first generation, and approximately 67 groups have so far debuted in the fifth generation.

NOTE: "No data" means that the ages of members of group could not be found.
This data is not proportional or meant to be compared for many reasons. The biggest of which is that the number of groups is unequal. The second reason is that many members are born the same year. For example, the youngest members, Rosé and Lisa, of third generation girl group BLACKPINK are both born in 1997, but only the maknae of the group would be counted—Lisa.

Debut Ages of 1st Gen K-pop Maknaes

Of these idols who debuted in the first generation, 54% were minors and .05% were 14 or under.

Debut Ages of 5th Gen K-pop Maknaes

Of these idols who debuted in the fifth generation, 40% were minors and 10% were 14 or under.

While a good portion of idols debut in their mid to late teens or early adulthood, they spend many years prior training and preparing for it.
This raises the question, Why are K-pop agencies debuting idols so young, and why do they begin training them at an even younger age?
