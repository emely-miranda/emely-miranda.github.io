Why are K-pop agencies debuting idols so young, and why do they begin training them at an even younger age?

Reasons

Group Lifespan

Let's examine BTS, who is, in popular opinion, the most successful and well-known K-pop group.

BTS debuted in 2013 with seven members -- RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V and Jungkook--and are still active after 11 years (though they are technically on hiatus due to South Korean military law).

The group had a seven-year contract. At their debut, Jin, the eldest member, was 21. The maknae, Jungkook, debuted at 15 after training for three years. In 2020, the members, now all in their 20s, with fantastic popularity and a Grammy nomination, renewed their contracts for another seven years.

The group will continue to bring large profits to their company. Companies are banking on idols who debut young with long contracts and are more likely to renew contracts—prolonging their lifespan.

Development

Before becoming idols, people are trainees. Being an idol requires a lot of physical labor, from practicing choreographies to improving vocals.

Take Rora from the fifth-generation girl group BABYMONSTER.

Her start in K-pop preceded her debut as a YG Entertainment artist. In 2017, when she was 9, she debuted in the kid's girl group U.SSO. YG invited Rora to audition; she got in and had been training since 2018. She debuted in 2023 at 15 and is the second youngest member.

"I saw the potential," CEO of YG Entertainment, Yang Hyun-suk, said. "I was very sure that if we work on her voice well, it will turn out great."

Younger people retain more information. They can learn choreography and songs quickly, tire out less and keep up with the changing times. Companies can shape trainees into the best performers, singers and dancers they can be.

Appearances

South Korea has a particular beauty standard. There is even a position for the group member who best fits the standard: the visual. The K-pop audience, especially the domestic audience, prefers youthful-looking idols even if they are in their 30s. Tying into the reasoning of the lifespan of groups, if an idol debuts at an early age, as time goes on, they still look young but are now more mature as they've reached their peak on the K-pop scene.

Soloist Sunmi was part of the second-generation K-pop group Wonder Girls and the groups' visual. She and the maknae debuted at the age of 15. Even now, at 32 years old, she is still extremely popular and has a loyal fanbase.
